A gentle case for adversarial validation

Before tuning another hyperparameter, ask whether your train and serve populations still recognize each other.

Adversarial validation is a plain diagnostic: train a classifier to distinguish two datasets. If it succeeds too easily, your evaluation may be measuring the wrong future.

Use It Early

Run the check when you create a dataset, not only after a launch fails. It is cheaper to fix sampling, label windows, and missingness before the modeling loop gets emotionally expensive.

Inspect the Features It Uses

The classifier is not the answer; its strongest signals are. They point toward drift, leakage, geography, device mix, catalog changes, or instrumentation differences.

Do Not Panic

Some mismatch is expected. The goal is to learn whether it affects the decision you are trying to make.
