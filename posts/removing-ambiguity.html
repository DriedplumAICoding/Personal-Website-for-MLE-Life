Leadership · Jan 22, 2026

The senior engineer's job is often removing ambiguity

Technical leadership is less about certainty than making uncertainty legible enough for a team to move.

Good engineering judgment often shows up as a clear paragraph, a small experiment, or a decision record that explains what would change our mind.

Name the Reversible Parts

Teams move faster when they know which choices are hard to unwind and which can be revisited after learning from production.

Separate Facts From Bets

A design doc gets more useful when it labels measurements, assumptions, tradeoffs, and open questions. It gives reviewers somewhere precise to help.

Leave Better Edges

The best projects create a boundary that the next team can understand without reconstructing every conversation that led there.
